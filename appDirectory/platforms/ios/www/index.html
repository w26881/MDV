Week 1

  1. Twitter could be used to authenticate to third party services. Use the service you already have a relationship with (Twitter) to authenticate to new services. It could also be used to authenticate payments. It is very convenient especially since I am sure we are all tired of filling in web forms and creating accounts for every site on the net.

  2. We need JSONP to facilitate cross-domain communication and resource sharing.

  3. Geolocation pairs well with many things from providing metadata for created media like photos to checking in at a location or allowing a service to know if you are in the vicinity of an affiliated physical establishment. Notifications allow third party services to push important updates and offerings to the device. Contacts allows easy access to your relationships and can leverage that access to provide actionable features.

  4. I hope to integrate maps, geolocation, camera, and contacts into my app for tracking relationships. It is like having a dossier on each of the people in your life.

  5. Some past 'lessons learned' regarding workflow as it relates to research, design, development, etc. are to keep it simple and taking every opportunity to be immersing myself in the subject matter and searching the internet for inspiration. I have very little time due to the circumstances of my life so I have to be vigilant in not wasting any of it. So any free moment I get, I am going through lessons, or troubleshooting or debugging, or just setting up a framework for when I do get some time.

Week 2

  1. I could clean up my HTML quite a bit I think and add alt text to any images and make sure everything is described appropriately. Many of the things that could be done in the interest of accessibility should be done anyway. It serves as a great way to look at your work and polish it even more.

  2. Wow, watching the use of screen readers was really a wake-up call for me. In the context of accessibility, clean, organized markup is king. I saw how the screen readers could not help at all on some sites due to missing headings and it kind of just left the user hanging with few options. I cannot imagine how frustrating that would be. It really made me think about organizing my code and gave me a new perspective.

  3. iPhone and iPad both have accessibility features built in. The features include, Voiceover, Dictation, Siri, Speak Selection, Zoom, Large Text and color inversion options. http://www.apple.com/accessibility/ios/

  4. TapTapSee for iOS is an application that lets the user tap the screen twice while pointing the camera at an object and the app uses Voiceover to tell the user what the object is. It can be a shirt or a 5 dollar bill and the app will distinguish between currency denominations. https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/taptapsee-blind-visually-impaired/id567635020?mt=8

  5. Sendero GPS LookAround is an app that uses GPS to determine city, street, cross street and nearby points of interest and then audibly alert the visually impaired user and inform them of this information. https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/sendero-gps-lookaround/id386831856?mt=8

Week 3

  1. What are the 'top selling' app categories in the U.S.? Japan? Great Britain?

    Looking at various statistics for the three countries in question it becomes apparent that “games” is the top app category across the board. Social networking and entertainment categories both seem to follow within the top 4 in all locations as well but games are king.

  2. Based on your three answers above, what might you infer about each of the markets listed?

    This data leads me to believe that all three markets value gaming and entertainment when it comes to mobile applications.

  3. What is the average cost and bandwidth limits per user (in general) in each of the three markets? How may this impact development?

    I found that the average costs for wireless data service were all around 60 dollars for 5 GB per month. This would impact development in that decisions would need to be made about whether or not the app would require connectivity to use it and optimizations would need to be made to prevent the app from being too data-hungry.

  4. And, finally, what would be your suggested strategy in project topic and global market choice? Feel free to have a first and second choice.

    My suggested strategy would be to develop a game and target all markets. The demand is there.
